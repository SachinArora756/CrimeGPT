BHARATIYA NAGARIK SURAKSHA SANHITA (BNSS) 2023 - KEY PROCEDURES

FIRST INFORMATION REPORT (FIR) - Section 173

1. Every information relating to commission of a cognizable offence, if given orally, shall be reduced to writing.
2. The substance shall be entered in a book kept by the officer in charge of the police station (Station Diary).
3. A copy of the FIR shall be given free of cost to the informant.
4. If the information discloses commission of a cognizable offence, the officer shall register the FIR.
5. Zero FIR: Any police station can register FIR regardless of jurisdiction. Must transfer to appropriate station within 15 days.
6. E-FIR: FIR can be registered through electronic communication. Physical copy must be obtained within 3 days.
7. Mandatory upload to state/national database within 24 hours.

ARREST PROCEDURES - Section 35

1. Every police officer may arrest any person who has committed a cognizable offence.
2. Person arrested shall be informed of grounds of arrest.
3. Right to inform a person of their choice about arrest.
4. Must be produced before magistrate within 24 hours.
5. No arrest for offences punishable with less than 3 years without prior permission of officer not below DSP rank.
6. Woman cannot be arrested after sunset and before sunrise except by a woman officer with prior permission of magistrate.

INVESTIGATION - Section 175-190

1. Investigation to be completed within 60 days (if imprisonment up to 3 years) or 90 days (if above 3 years).
2. If not completed, preliminary chargesheet must be filed.
3. Statements of witnesses to be recorded. Electronic recording permitted.
4. All evidence to be collected, sealed, and preserved with chain of custody documentation.
5. Forensic evidence must be sent to laboratory within 7 days of collection.

CHARGESHEET - Section 193

1. After completion of investigation, officer shall submit final report (chargesheet) to magistrate.
2. Must include: names of parties, nature of information, names of persons found guilty, whether accused arrested or released on bail.
3. All documents and statements recorded during investigation to be annexed.
4. Supplementary chargesheet can be filed if further evidence collected.

BAIL - Section 478-495

1. Bailable offences: bail as matter of right.
2. Non-bailable offences: bail at discretion of court.
3. Default bail: If investigation not completed within stipulated time (60/90 days), accused entitled to bail.
4. Anticipatory bail: May be granted by Sessions Court or High Court.
5. Conditions may include surrender of passport, appearance at police station, etc.

SEARCH AND SEIZURE - Section 185-190

1. Search warrant to be issued by magistrate.
2. Search to be conducted in presence of two independent witnesses from the locality.
3. All seized items to be inventoried with description, quantity, and identifying marks.
4. Receipt to be given to the person from whom items are seized.
5. Digital evidence: Electronic devices to be seized with hash value computation and forensic imaging.
6. Chain of custody must be maintained for all seized items.

VICTIM RIGHTS - Section 360

1. Right to be heard at any proceeding under this Sanhita.
2. Right to engage an advocate of choice.
3. Right to receive information about status of investigation.
4. Victim compensation scheme.
5. Protection from intimidation and threats.
6. Mandatory victim impact assessment in serious offences.
