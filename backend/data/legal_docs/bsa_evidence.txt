BHARATIYA SAKSHYA ADHINIYAM (BSA) 2023 - LAW OF EVIDENCE

ELECTRONIC EVIDENCE - Section 61-65

Section 61: Contents of electronic records may be proved by production of output.
- Printout from computer
- Data stored in optical/magnetic media
- Computer-generated output

Section 63: Admissibility of electronic records
- Electronic records are admissible as evidence.
- Must be accompanied by certificate under Section 63(4).
- Certificate must identify the electronic record, describe manner of production, and give details of device.

Section 65: Special provisions for electronic evidence
- Hash value must be computed at time of seizure.
- Chain of custody must be maintained.
- Forensic examination report from government-approved laboratory.
- Integrity of evidence must be demonstrable.

DOCUMENTARY EVIDENCE - Section 57-60

Section 57: Primary evidence
- Document itself produced for inspection of court.
- In case of electronic records, any output from computer is primary evidence.

Section 58: Secondary evidence
- Certified copies, copies made from original by mechanical process.
- Oral accounts by person who examined the original.
- Admissible when original lost, in possession of adverse party, or public document.

WITNESS EXAMINATION - Section 137-155

Section 137: Examination-in-chief
- Examination of witness by party who calls them.
- Leading questions not permitted except on undisputed matters.

Section 138: Cross-examination
- Examination by opposing party.
- Leading questions permitted.
- Must relate to relevant facts.

Section 139: Re-examination
- By party who called the witness.
- Limited to matters raised in cross-examination.
- No new matter without leave of court.

ADMISSIONS AND CONFESSIONS - Section 15-23

Section 15: Admission defined
- Statement suggesting inference about fact in issue.
- Relevant when made by party to proceeding.

Section 21: Confession to police
- Not admissible against accused.
- Exception: Leading to discovery of fact (Section 22).

Section 22: Discovery statements
- Portion of confession leading to discovery of fact may be proved.
- Only the portion distinctly relating to fact discovered.

EXPERT EVIDENCE - Section 45

Section 45: Expert opinion relevant
- On foreign law, science, art, handwriting, finger impressions, electronic evidence.
- Expert must have special skill through practice, observation, or study.
- Court may summon expert.

PRESUMPTIONS

Section 79: Presumption about documents 30 years old
- Document produced from proper custody and appearing genuine.

Section 81: Presumption about electronic records
- Electronic records in 5 years or more from proper custody.
- Presumed genuine if signature/seal verified.

Section 85A: Presumption about electronic agreements
- Electronic agreements presumed genuine.
- Court may presume electronic record with digital signature to be authentic.

BURDEN OF PROOF - Section 101-106

Section 101: On whom burden lies
- Who asserts must prove.
- Prosecution bears burden in criminal cases (beyond reasonable doubt).

Section 105: Burden of proving exceptions
- Burden on accused to prove circumstances bringing case within exceptions.
- Standard: Preponderance of probability (not beyond reasonable doubt).
