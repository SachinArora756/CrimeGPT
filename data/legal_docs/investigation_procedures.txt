DETAILED INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES AND GUIDELINES
===================================================

CHAPTER I - FORENSIC INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES

Section 1 - Forensic Science Laboratory (FSL) Submission
(1) All physical evidence shall be submitted to the FSL with a proper forwarding letter.
(2) The forwarding letter shall contain: case number, FIR details, description of exhibits, specific questions to be answered by the expert.
(3) Each exhibit shall be packed separately, sealed with the IO's seal, and labeled.
(4) Biological samples shall be maintained at appropriate temperature during transport.
(5) A receipt shall be obtained from FSL for all exhibits submitted.
(6) FSL reports shall be obtained within the prescribed timeline and attached to chargesheet.
Timeline: Follow up within 15 days; escalate if report not received within 30 days.

Section 2 - DNA Evidence Procedures
(1) DNA samples shall be collected by trained personnel only.
(2) Reference samples: buccal swabs from suspect and victim (with consent/court order).
(3) Crime scene samples: blood, semen, hair roots, skin cells, saliva.
(4) Avoid cross-contamination: change gloves between samples, use sterile equipment.
(5) Storage: refrigerate at 2-8 degrees C, never freeze liquid blood samples.
(6) Submit to DNA laboratory within 72 hours of collection.
(7) DNA profiling results shall be matched against National DNA Database (once operational).
Requirements: Trained personnel, sterile collection, refrigerated storage, 72-hour submission.

Section 3 - Ballistic Analysis Procedures
(1) Firearm recovery: note exact position, photograph in situ, handle with care.
(2) Test firing: 3-5 rounds for comparison purposes.
(3) Submit: recovered firearm, spent cartridges from scene, bullet/projectile from victim/scene.
(4) Ballistic expert will determine: match between firearm and bullet/cartridge, range of firing, direction of firing.
(5) Serial number restoration if obliterated.
(6) Gunshot residue (GSR) samples from suspect's hands within 6 hours of incident.
Timeline: GSR collection within 6 hours; firearm submission within 24 hours.

Section 4 - Toxicology Procedures
(1) In cases of suspected poisoning: collect vomit, blood, urine, viscera during post-mortem.
(2) Preserve in appropriate containers (fluoride for blood alcohol, plain for drugs).
(3) Control samples from same batch of containers.
(4) Submit with specific questions regarding suspected substance.
(5) Chemical examiner's report is essential for poison cases.
Timeline: Viscera to be preserved and sent to Chemical Examiner within 24 hours of post-mortem.

Section 5 - Post-Mortem and Medico-Legal Procedures
(1) In cases of unnatural death: inquest by police officer (Section 194 BNSS).
(2) Body to be sent for post-mortem examination with proper identification.
(3) Two constables shall escort the body; IO shall attend post-mortem if possible.
(4) Post-mortem request shall specify: cause of death required, specific questions, injuries to be documented.
(5) Videography of post-mortem mandatory for offences of 7+ years.
(6) Additional samples (viscera, blood, swabs) as directed by doctor.
(7) Post-mortem report to be collected personally and discrepancies clarified immediately.
Requirements: Inquest report, identification, escort, specific questions, videography for serious cases.

CHAPTER II - DIGITAL EVIDENCE AND CYBER INVESTIGATION

Section 6 - Electronic Device Seizure Protocol
(1) Document device state (on/off, screen content, running programs).
(2) Photograph the device in situ with visible serial numbers.
(3) Mobile phones: if ON, enable airplane mode first; place in Faraday bag if available.
(4) Computers: record volatile data if trained; otherwise note screen and pull power.
(5) Record all accessories (chargers, cables, memory cards).
(6) Complete seizure memo with device details (make, model, IMEI, serial number).
(7) Seal with IO's seal; have two witnesses sign.
(8) DO NOT attempt to access, browse, or unlock devices without forensic tools.
Requirements: Document state, photograph, Faraday bag for phones, proper seizure memo, DO NOT access.

Section 7 - Digital Forensic Imaging
(1) Forensic imaging shall be conducted by trained cyber forensic examiner.
(2) Write-blocker shall be used when connecting storage media.
(3) Bit-by-bit forensic image shall be created of all storage media.
(4) Hash values (MD5 and SHA-256) of original and image shall be computed and compared.
(5) All subsequent examination shall be performed on the forensic copy, NEVER on the original.
(6) Hash value verification report shall be prepared as per BSA Section 65.
(7) Chain of custody for digital evidence shall include hash verification at each transfer.
Requirements: Write-blocker, bit-by-bit image, hash verification, examine copy only.

Section 8 - Call Detail Records (CDR) and IP Data
(1) CDR requisition to telecom provider under proper legal authority.
(2) Include: target numbers, date range (not exceeding case-relevant period), purpose.
(3) Tower dump requests: specify tower IDs and time window.
(4) IPDR (IP Detail Records) for internet activity.
(5) Analysis shall include: call patterns, tower locations, movement mapping.
(6) CDR analysis report with charts and timelines for chargesheet.
(7) BSA Section 63 certificate required from telecom provider for court admissibility.
Requirements: Proper authority, limited scope, Section 63 BSA certificate for admissibility.

Section 9 - Social Media and Online Evidence
(1) Screenshots with URL visible and timestamp (use system clock showing time).
(2) Source code/page info for authentication.
(3) Request to platform for account data (through proper legal channels/MLAT if foreign platform).
(4) Hash screenshots immediately.
(5) Section 63 BSA certificate for all electronic evidence produced.
(6) URL archival (Wayback Machine, archive.today) for web pages that may be deleted.
(7) Email headers preserved for IP trail.
Requirements: Screenshots with timestamp, source code, hash, Section 63 certificate.

Section 10 - CCTV Footage Collection
(1) Identify all CCTV cameras in relevant area (scene, approach routes, suspect routes).
(2) Issue notice to camera owners for preservation of footage.
(3) Collect within 48-72 hours before automatic overwriting.
(4) Collect in original format (do not accept converted/compressed versions if possible).
(5) Note camera time vs actual time (offset calculation).
(6) Hash value of collected footage.
(7) Prepare frame-by-frame analysis of relevant portions.
(8) Section 63 BSA certificate from the camera operator/owner.
Timeline: Collect within 48-72 hours; most systems overwrite within 7-30 days.

CHAPTER III - SPECIAL INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES

Section 11 - Investigation of Sexual Offences
(1) Female officer shall record statement of victim if available.
(2) Statement to be recorded at residence of victim or place of victim's choice.
(3) Audio-video recording mandatory (BNSS Section 531).
(4) Medical examination within 24 hours by registered medical practitioner; consent of victim required (or guardian if minor).
(5) MLC (Medico Legal Certificate) in prescribed format.
(6) DNA evidence collection during medical examination.
(7) Victim's clothes and suspect's clothes collected separately in paper bags.
(8) One-stop centre referral for counseling and legal aid.
(9) Statements of all persons at scene/aware of incident.
(10) Electronic evidence: messages, calls, social media interactions.
(11) Section 164 CrPC/183 BNSS statement before Magistrate as soon as possible.
Requirements: Female officer, victim's location, audio-video, medical within 24 hours, DNA, Magistrate statement.

Section 12 - Investigation of Economic Offences
(1) Secure financial documents immediately (bank statements, ledgers, vouchers).
(2) Account freezing order from competent authority if necessary.
(3) Engage Chartered Accountant for audit trail.
(4) Collect digital evidence (accounting software data, email correspondence).
(5) Analysis of fund flow through multiple accounts.
(6) Property identification and attachment proceedings if applicable.
(7) Coordination with ED, SFIO, Income Tax as applicable.
(8) Expert evidence for complex financial instruments.
Requirements: Document preservation, account freeze, CA audit, fund flow, property attachment.

Section 13 - Homicide Investigation Special Steps
(1) Scene of Crime Officer (SOCO) mandatory for homicide scenes.
(2) 360-degree videography of scene.
(3) Blood spatter analysis documentation.
(4) Identification of victim (if unknown): photographs, fingerprints, dental records, DNA.
(5) Last seen alive evidence.
(6) Motive investigation: financial, personal enmity, passion, professional.
(7) Weapon recovery and matching.
(8) Alibi verification of suspects.
(9) CCTV and electronic trail mapping.
(10) Post-mortem with full toxicology.
(11) Circumstantial evidence chain construction.
Requirements: SOCO, videography, identification, motive, weapon, alibi, electronic trail.

Section 14 - Investigation of Kidnapping/Missing Persons
(1) Immediate registration of FIR without waiting 24 hours (for minors: immediate).
(2) Flash message to all police stations in district.
(3) Check CCTV on all routes from last known location.
(4) CDR analysis of victim's phone (last tower, last calls).
(5) Social media check for online contacts.
(6) Passport and border check alerts.
(7) Check with known offenders in area.
(8) Broadcast on police wireless.
(9) For children: check with CWC, Child Helpline, known traffickers.
(10) Ransom call tracing if applicable (coordinate with technical cell).
Requirements: Immediate FIR, flash message, CCTV, CDR, alerts, no 24-hour waiting period.

CHAPTER IV - WITNESS PROTECTION AND VICTIM RIGHTS

Section 15 - Witness Protection Measures
Categories of protection (based on threat assessment):
Category A (highest): Personal security, relocation, identity change
Category B: Secure housing, regular escort, identity protection in records
Category C: Court protection (screen, video link testimony, in-camera proceedings)
(1) Application to competent authority with threat assessment report.
(2) Identity of protected witness sealed in court records.
(3) Video-link testimony allowed under BNSS Section 530.
(4) Witness deposition outside court hours if necessary.
(5) Travel expenses and loss of income compensation.

Section 16 - Victim Rights During Investigation
(1) Right to be informed of progress of investigation (periodic updates).
(2) Right to be heard before closure/final report.
(3) Right to file protest petition if dissatisfied with investigation.
(4) Right to victim compensation (interim and final).
(5) Right to legal representation at State expense.
(6) Right to medical treatment at State expense.
(7) Right to privacy and dignity.
(8) Right not to be re-victimized during investigation.
(9) Special rights for child victims (POCSO Act procedures).

Section 17 - Juvenile Justice Procedures
(1) Person below 18 years is a juvenile/child in conflict with law.
(2) No arrest: only apprehension and production before Juvenile Justice Board.
(3) No handcuffing of juveniles.
(4) No detention in police station: sent to observation home.
(5) Age determination: matriculation certificate, birth certificate, ossification test.
(6) Social investigation report by Probation Officer.
(7) Maximum detention: 3 years in special home.
(8) Exception: juveniles aged 16-18 for heinous offences may be tried as adults after JJB assessment.
Requirements: No arrest/handcuffing, JJB production, observation home, age verification.

CHAPTER V - CASE CLOSURE AND FILING PROCEDURES

Section 18 - Final Report Types
(1) Chargesheet (Section 193 BNSS): When evidence sufficient to prosecute.
(2) Closure Report (FR - Final Report): When offence not made out or evidence insufficient.
  Types of closure:
  - False case: FIR found to be false
  - Mistake of fact: Civil nature or no offence disclosed
  - Insufficient evidence: Unable to collect sufficient evidence
  - Undetected: Offender not identified
  - Non-cognizable: Case found to be non-cognizable
(3) The closure report must be submitted to Magistrate who may accept or reject.
(4) Complainant has right to oppose closure and request further investigation.
(5) Magistrate may order further investigation even after closure.

Section 19 - Supplementary Chargesheet
(1) After filing initial chargesheet, if further evidence is obtained, a supplementary chargesheet may be filed.
(2) Additional accused may be added through supplementary chargesheet.
(3) Additional evidence/witnesses discovered after filing.
(4) No time limit for supplementary chargesheet but should be filed before commencement of trial.

Section 20 - Evidence Preservation Post-Chargesheet
(1) Physical evidence retained in malkhana (evidence room) until case disposal.
(2) Periodic verification of evidence integrity.
(3) After final disposal: non-objectionable property returned to owner; objectionable property destroyed under court order.
(4) Digital evidence preserved in original format with hash verification.
(5) Photographs and video recordings preserved permanently in case record.
Requirements: Malkhana storage, periodic verification, hash-based integrity for digital evidence.
