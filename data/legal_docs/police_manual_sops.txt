POLICE INVESTIGATION MANUAL - STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES
==============================================================

CHAPTER I - CRIME SCENE MANAGEMENT

Section 1 - Initial Response
(1) Upon receiving information of a cognizable offence, the duty officer shall immediately dispatch a team to the crime scene.
(2) The first responder shall: secure the scene perimeter, prevent contamination, note all persons present, record time of arrival.
(3) A crime scene log shall be maintained recording all persons entering and exiting the scene.
Procedure: Information received -> Team dispatched -> Scene secured -> Perimeter established -> Log maintained.

Section 2 - Scene Preservation
(1) The crime scene shall be cordoned off using barrier tape or physical barriers.
(2) No person shall enter the inner cordon without the permission of the investigating officer.
(3) All entry and exit shall be through a single designated path.
(4) Weather conditions and their potential effect on evidence shall be recorded.
(5) Fragile or perishable evidence shall be prioritized for collection.
Requirements: Cordoning, single entry path, weather recording, prioritized collection.

Section 3 - Scene Documentation
(1) The scene shall be documented through: written notes, sketches/diagrams, photography, and videography.
(2) Photographs shall be taken: overall views from multiple angles, mid-range contextual shots, close-up detail shots with and without scale.
(3) Video recording shall capture a continuous walkthrough of the entire scene.
(4) A scaled diagram shall be prepared showing relative positions of evidence, bodies, furniture, and other significant items.
Requirements: Notes, sketches, photos (overview/mid/close-up), video walkthrough, scaled diagram.

Section 4 - Evidence Search Patterns
(1) Evidence search shall be conducted systematically using one of the following patterns:
  - Strip/Line Search: Team walks in parallel lines across the scene
  - Grid Search: Strip search repeated at 90 degrees
  - Spiral Search: From outside inward or inside outward
  - Zone/Quadrant Search: Area divided into sections, each searched independently
(2) The search pattern shall be selected based on scene type, size, and terrain.
(3) All items of potential evidentiary value shall be marked before collection.

CHAPTER II - EVIDENCE COLLECTION AND HANDLING

Section 5 - General Principles
(1) Evidence shall be collected only by trained personnel wearing appropriate PPE.
(2) Each item of evidence shall be individually packaged, sealed, and labeled.
(3) Labels shall include: case number, exhibit number, description, date/time of collection, location found, collector's name and badge number.
(4) Chain of custody shall be initiated at the point of collection.
(5) Contamination prevention: new gloves for each item, tools cleaned between uses.
Requirements: PPE, individual packaging, proper labeling, chain of custody from collection.

Section 6 - Biological Evidence
(1) Blood samples: use sterile cotton swabs, air dry before packaging, store at 4 degrees C.
(2) DNA samples: use buccal swabs for reference, avoid contamination, store in paper not plastic.
(3) Hair and fibres: use forceps, place in paper folds within sealed envelopes.
(4) Body fluids: collect on sterile substrates, air dry, do not expose to heat or sunlight.
(5) All biological evidence shall be submitted to FSL within 72 hours.
Timeline: Submit to Forensic Science Laboratory within 72 hours.

Section 7 - Digital Evidence
(1) Electronic devices shall not be powered on/off at the scene unless by trained digital forensic examiner.
(2) Mobile phones: if on, keep powered (to preserve volatile data); if off, keep off. Place in Faraday bag if available.
(3) Computers: photograph screen, document running programs, do not shut down normally — pull power cord for desktops, keep laptops powered.
(4) Storage media: handle by edges, protect from magnetic fields, static, and physical damage.
(5) Document all passwords, PINs visible at scene.
(6) Hash values (MD5/SHA-256) shall be computed for all digital evidence at the earliest opportunity.
Requirements: Do not power on/off, Faraday bag for phones, hash values computed, forensic imaging.

Section 8 - Firearm Evidence
(1) Note position and direction of barrel before moving.
(2) Do not insert anything into the barrel.
(3) Unload safely if loaded, document number of rounds.
(4) Package in rigid container to prevent accidental discharge.
(5) Collect spent cartridges, note positions on diagram.
(6) Record serial numbers, make, model, caliber.
(7) Submit for ballistic analysis along with recovered projectiles.

Section 9 - Fingerprint Evidence
(1) Latent prints: develop using appropriate powders or chemical methods.
(2) Patent prints (visible): photograph before any lifting attempt.
(3) Plastic prints (impressions): photograph with oblique lighting.
(4) Use of cyanoacrylate fuming for non-porous surfaces.
(5) All lifted prints shall be compared against the database (AFIS).
(6) Elimination prints of all persons legitimately present shall be collected.

Section 10 - Chain of Custody
(1) Chain of custody documentation shall be maintained from the moment evidence is collected until its production in court.
(2) Every transfer of custody shall be recorded with: date, time, from whom, to whom, purpose of transfer.
(3) Evidence shall be stored in a secure evidence room with restricted access.
(4) Access to evidence room shall be logged.
(5) Any break in the chain of custody may render evidence inadmissible under BSA Section 65.
(6) Digital evidence chain shall also document hash value verification at each transfer.
Requirements: Continuous documentation, logged access, hash verification for digital evidence.

CHAPTER III - STATEMENT RECORDING

Section 11 - Recording Statements Under Section 179 BNSS
(1) Statements shall be recorded in the language of the witness and translated if necessary.
(2) Audio-video recording shall be used wherever facilities are available (mandatory under BNSS Section 179(4)).
(3) Questions shall be open-ended, non-leading, and non-suggestive.
(4) The witness shall be cautioned that making false statements is punishable.
(5) Vulnerable witnesses (children, persons with disabilities) shall be questioned in presence of appropriate support person.
(6) Statement recording shall take place in a comfortable environment, preferably at the police station or witness's residence.
Requirements: Witness language, audio-video recording, non-leading questions, vulnerable witness support.

Section 12 - Recording Dying Declaration
(1) A dying declaration is a statement made by a person who is about to die or is under expectation of death.
(2) It is admissible without oath under BSA Section 26.
(3) Where possible, it shall be recorded by a Magistrate.
(4) If recorded by a police officer: the mental fitness of the declarant shall be certified by a doctor if available.
(5) Questions and answers shall be recorded verbatim.
(6) The declarant's thumb impression or signature shall be obtained if possible.
(7) Time of commencement and conclusion of recording shall be noted.
(8) The certificate of the doctor regarding fitness to make statement shall be attached.
Requirements: Magistrate preferred, doctor certification of fitness, verbatim recording, time noted.

Section 13 - Confession Before Magistrate (Section 183 BNSS)
(1) The Magistrate shall explain to the accused that he is not bound to make a confession.
(2) The accused shall be given time to reflect (minimum 24 hours between arrest and confession).
(3) The Magistrate shall satisfy himself that the confession is voluntary.
(4) Questions shall be put to ascertain voluntariness: any inducement, threat, promise, physical violence.
(5) The confession shall be recorded in the words of the accused.
(6) The Magistrate shall certify that it was made voluntarily.
Requirements: Voluntariness ensured, 24-hour reflection, Magistrate certification.

CHAPTER IV - ARREST AND CUSTODY PROCEDURES

Section 14 - Pre-Arrest Checklist
Before making an arrest, the officer shall consider:
(1) Whether the offence is cognizable
(2) Whether there is reasonable suspicion or credible information
(3) Whether arrest is necessary (Section 35 BNSS criteria)
(4) Whether a notice of appearance would suffice instead
(5) Whether the arrested person falls under any special category (woman, juvenile, senior citizen)
(6) Whether proper documentation (arrest memo, rights card) is ready
Assessment: Necessity of arrest must be documented; non-arrest alternatives preferred for offences up to 7 years.

Section 15 - Arrest Execution Procedure
(1) Identify yourself clearly (name, rank, station)
(2) Inform the person of the offence and grounds of arrest
(3) Inform the person of their rights (legal counsel, inform family, medical examination)
(4) Prepare arrest memo with witness attestation
(5) Search the person and record personal belongings
(6) Note any pre-existing injuries on the body
(7) Allow the person to contact family/lawyer
(8) Transport to police station safely
(9) Entry in station diary
(10) Medical examination at the earliest
(11) Production before Magistrate within 24 hours
Timeline: Medical examination at earliest; Magistrate production within 24 hours.

Section 16 - Custody Rights of Accused
(1) Access to legal counsel
(2) Inform family of arrest and location of custody
(3) Medical examination every 48 hours
(4) No physical or mental torture
(5) Access to basic necessities (food, water, hygiene, sleep)
(6) Female accused: custody under female officer, search only by female officer
(7) Juvenile: separate from adult accused, inform Juvenile Justice Board

CHAPTER V - INVESTIGATION PLANNING

Section 17 - Case Strategy
(1) Upon registration of FIR, the investigating officer shall prepare an investigation plan covering:
  - Evidence to be collected (physical, digital, documentary, testimonial)
  - Witnesses to be examined
  - Technical/scientific examination required
  - Timeline for key steps
  - Resources required
(2) The investigation plan shall be reviewed and updated as new information emerges.
(3) For serious offences (7+ years imprisonment), the plan shall be reviewed by the supervising officer.

Section 18 - Investigative Steps Priority
Priority order for standard investigation:
(1) Secure and process crime scene
(2) Record FIR and initial statements
(3) Collect and preserve perishable evidence
(4) Identify and examine witnesses
(5) Collect medical/forensic evidence
(6) Digital evidence preservation and analysis
(7) Suspect identification and arrest (if warranted)
(8) Post-arrest investigation (confessions, discoveries, TIP)
(9) Corroborative evidence collection
(10) Chargesheet preparation

Section 19 - Coordination with Other Agencies
(1) Forensic Science Laboratory (FSL): For analysis of physical evidence
(2) Cyber Crime Cell: For digital evidence and electronic trail
(3) Medico-Legal: For injury documentation, cause of death, sexual assault examination
(4) Revenue/Land Records: For property disputes
(5) Bank/Financial institutions: For financial fraud cases
(6) Telecom providers: For CDR, IPDR, tower location data
(7) Inter-state coordination: Through CCTNS portal
Requirements: Document all agency requests and responses.

CHAPTER VI - DOCUMENTATION AND REPORTING

Section 20 - Case Diary Maintenance
(1) Case diary shall be maintained daily during active investigation.
(2) Each entry shall contain: date, time, actions taken, persons met, evidence collected, observations.
(3) The case diary shall be written by the investigating officer personally.
(4) It shall be submitted to the supervising officer for perusal weekly.
(5) Court may summon case diary but it is NOT evidence — used only as aid for inquiry/trial.
Requirements: Daily, personal, time-stamped, supervised weekly.

Section 21 - Status Reports
(1) Weekly status reports shall be submitted to the supervising officer.
(2) Monthly reports shall be submitted to the Superintendent of Police.
(3) Reports shall include: progress made, pending steps, expected timeline, difficulties encountered.
(4) Cases pending beyond prescribed timelines shall be flagged for special review.

Section 22 - Chargesheet Preparation
The chargesheet (Section 193 BNSS) shall include:
(1) Cover page with case particulars
(2) FIR copy
(3) Statement of complainant
(4) Statements of witnesses (Section 179 BNSS)
(5) Confession/admission statements (if before Magistrate)
(6) Panchnamas (inquest, scene, seizure, arrest)
(7) Medical/forensic/scientific reports
(8) Electronic evidence with Section 63 BSA certificates
(9) List of documents relied upon
(10) List of witnesses to be examined
(11) Previous conviction record of accused (if any)
(12) Property list and custody chart
Requirements: All above documents mandatory for a complete chargesheet.
